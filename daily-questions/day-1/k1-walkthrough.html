K1 Aristocrat walkthrough

A K1 Aristocrat is a monoalphabetic substitution where the plaintext alphabet is built from a keyword. Here's the method; then try it live on the example below.

The method

Five moves that crack almost any Aristocrat

  1. Count the letters. Fill the frequency table. The most common cipher letters are almost always E, T, A, O, I, N.
  2. Attack the short words. A one-letter word is A or I. Three-letter words are often THE or AND, a huge head start.
  3. Use contact patterns. A letter that appears right before an apostrophe + one letter is usually N'T or 'S. Doubles (LL, SS, OO) narrow things fast.
  4. Spread your guesses. Every letter you place rules others out. Pencil them into the replacement row and let the words half-form.
  5. Read the keyword. As the alphabet fills in, the keyword often appears in your replacement row; confirm the last few letters from it.

Type a guessed plaintext letter under each cipher letter. Auto-advance moves you along; arrow keys and Backspace let you jump around. Hit Check answer when you think you've got it.
