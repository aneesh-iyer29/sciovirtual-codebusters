K2 Aristocrat walkthrough

A K2 Aristocrat is the mirror image of a K1: the ciphertext alphabet is the one built from a keyword. The solving moves are the same, but the frequency table gives you an extra gift.

K1 vs K2

Same solve, different keyword hiding spot

Everything from the K1 walkthrough still applies: count letters, break the short words, use contact patterns. The difference is where the keyword lives:

  • K1: the keyword is embedded in the plaintext alphabet.
  • K2: the keyword is embedded in the ciphertext alphabet, so when you write your recovered plaintext under A B C … in the frequency table, the keyword tends to surface as a run of consecutive letters.
  1. Solve a handful of letters the usual way, enough to get traction on the replacement row.
  2. Watch the replacement row. In a K2 you'll often see the keyword appear straight across the table; use it to fill the rest of the alphabet.
  3. Finish and verify by reading the full quote back; it should be grammatical, not just plausible.
